JOHTO REGION

Johto Region — Brief Lore

Johto is the "old world" region west of Kanto, inspired by Japan’s Kansai area. It emphasizes history, tradition, and strong spiritual ties between people and Pokémon.

Key Themes

  • Tradition & History: Cities such as Ecruteak preserve ancient customs and sacred sites.
  • Nature & Spirituality: Johto balances progress with respect for nature and Pokémon.
  • Connection to Kanto: Linked by travel and the shared Pokémon League, Johto and Kanto are closely tied.

Legendary Mythos

Johto is home to the region’s major legends: Ho-Oh (guardian of the skies) and Lugia (guardian of the seas). Ho-Oh revived the three Legendary Beasts — Entei, Raikou, and Suicune — after the Brass (Burned) Tower fire.

Important Places

  • Ecruteak City: Ancient towers, long-standing rituals, and the heart of Johto’s myths.
  • Goldenrod City: The region’s commercial and technological hub — a contrast to Johto’s older towns.
  • Indigo Plateau: The Pokémon League destination trainers aim for after collecting Johto’s eight badges.

Summary

In short: Johto is a region of legends and balance — where history, sacred places, and classic Pokémon myths shape its identity alongside growing modernity.
